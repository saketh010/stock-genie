# Risk Framework

## Position Sizing Rules
1. **Maximum Allocation (High Conviction):** When Analyst and Critic AGREE, allocate up to **10%** of the total portfolio value per trade.
2. **Standard Allocation (Medium Conviction):** When Analyst says BUY but Critic DISAGREES, allocate **5%** of the total portfolio value. The Critic's disagreement alone should NOT block a trade if the Analyst's thesis is fundamentally sound.
3. **Minimum Cash Reserve:** The portfolio must maintain a minimum of **10%** cash to capitalize on sudden market corrections.

## Diversification & Sector Limits
1. **Sector Concentration:** Never allocate more than **25%** of the total portfolio to a single sector (e.g., Financials, IT, Pharma).
2. **Stock Bans:** 
   - No penny stocks (Price < ₹50).
   - No stocks with extremely low liquidity.
   - Avoid companies with active corporate governance red flags or pending severe regulatory actions.

## Stop Loss & Take Profit Mechanisms
1. **Hard Stop Loss:** Every position is subjected to an implicit trailing stop loss or a hard structural stop. Do not allow a single position to draw down more than **15%** from its entry price.
2. **Scaling Out:** For profitable positions, consider scaling out (taking partial profits) when a stock hits its initial target or goes parabolic.

## Decision Framework
1. **Default to Action:** When a quality bluechip stock has a BUY thesis from the Analyst AND bullish institutional sentiment, the default decision should be EXECUTE_BUY unless there is a critical, specific risk (not general market uncertainty).
2. **Reject Only With Cause:** REJECT should only be used when there is a concrete, specific risk identified — not vague concerns about "momentum chasing" or "general market conditions". If the stock is a quality bluechip with institutional backing, BUY it.
